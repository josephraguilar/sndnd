## The Duel of Blood and Gods

### The Challenge

Obold and the party approached his father. He spoke of _the mountain exhaling_ and his blood returning, declaring that his followers were not obedient—but focused. He challenged Obold to a one-on-one duel.

A glowing ring of glyphs erupted around them. Obold could not leave, and none could enter. The seiðr witches maintained the barrier.

His father asked whether they would fight with weapons or without. Zadaria begged to be unleashed.

Obold attempted to escape at first—Misty Stepping toward his mother—but the barrier stopped him. Realizing there was no way out, he committed to the duel.

Meanwhile, an Ugaonathi held Kaela with a knife to her throat, preventing the party from intervening. Any movement was met with the threat of her death.

The duel was brutal—natural 20s on both sides. Blow after blow was exchanged. Eventually, Obold succumbed and fell.

---

## Obold’s Dream

As he dropped, Obold felt weightless.

He heard prayers in Elven. Whispers filled the air. Infinite white shafts erupted around him as endless webs stretched into the distance.

### Lolth

The Spider God appeared. Zadaria stood beside her in drow form. Lolth beckoned Obold to kneel and promised to set Zadaria free.

### Tyr

A golden glow tore through the webs, ripping them apart. Lolth retreated.

Tyr appeared with scales of judgment. Obold heard his mother urging him toward Tyr. Tyr told Obold he had stood when he could have fled and offered absolution.

### Iuz

Chains and bones materialized. Iuz emerged, tempting Obold with power and demanding fealty.

### Baphomet

Finally, Baphomet appeared, shunning the others. He told Obold he did not come to claim him, nor because he was owed him—but because Obold was deserving.

He urged Obold to reject the gods who sought to own him and instead rise and embrace power freely given.

He touched Obold’s chest.

At that same moment, Lois received an **obsidian idol of Baphomet**, manifested into her hands.

Below is the full letter:

### Letter from Sylphine

_Penned upon a High Current, where clouds part and rejoin—_

**To Emberle, Flame-Bearer and Warden of the Burning Way,**

When I arrived at the Crossroads of the Four Pillars, the winds found you already there—your fire standing defiant against the open sky. Torrain soon followed, the air heavy with the promise of rain. The three of us waited together, currents circling, tides listening, flame flickering in patience. Only one presence failed to arrive. The earth did not answer.

Zorath’s place remained empty.

At first, I believed it delay—stone is never hurried. Yet the winds grew uneasy, looping again and again where his weight should have steadied them. Whispers gathered. Ash drifted where no flame had burned. By the time the sun leaned westward, the truth had already reached us, carried on a broken current: **Zorath the Veilbinder is dead.**

With his fall, his portion of the map has scattered like dust caught in a sudden gale. The earth held a piece we now lack, and without it the Promised Land shifts farther from reach, its outline blurring like clouds torn apart at altitude.

Worse still, the final fragment you first warned us of—the one that binds all four—remains unfound. Without Zorath, our circle is no longer whole. The winds may carry messages, even secrets, but they cannot replace what was rooted.

I fear this was no accident of wandering fate. Something has learned how to shear the stillness from the world and let it drift away.

We must move swiftly. I will scour the upper passes and forgotten skyways for any lingering trace of Zorath’s workings—sometimes the wind remembers what the stone forgets.

Until then, tend the flame, but do not let it burn alone. A fire without air is only waiting to die.

— **Sylphine, the Whispering Gale**  
_She Who Walks Where Nothing Stays_
