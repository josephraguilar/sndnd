## AI Summary

### The Hag's Lair

The party entered the lair carrying the wounds of the pocket dimension's lower level. [[campaign/npcs/Rotten Agnis Worm Wiggle\|Agnus the Maw]] was not what any of them had expected. She clung to the far wall like something that had forgotten it was supposed to walk upright — a horrific figure of wood and sinew and spider-angles, her limbs too long and bending the wrong way. She did not come down to meet them.

With her was a giant raven that occupied the room with the weight of a horse, and an ent — ancient, enormous, the kind of thing that could end the fight by itself.

The party opened up. Ranged shots, spells, opening attacks — the fight began in a burst while everyone read the room. Some went for the hag. Others went for the raven.

[[campaign/players/Obold\|Obold]] had an idea. He pulled [[campaign/players/Thorin\|Thorin]] aside long enough to borrow the magic rope — the kind that clings automatically to whatever it is thrown at — and then misty-stepped directly onto the raven's back. He got the rope around it. He was now attached to a giant, furious raven in an enclosed lair.

A turn or two into the fight, [[campaign/players/Thorin\|Thorin]] pointed at the ent and banished it. It was gone. He held that concentration for the rest of the fight while still managing to support the rest of the party from range.

The raven launched itself upward with Obold clinging to its back, attacking members of the party as it climbed. Eventually it found the ceiling — or the pocket dimension's equivalent of sky — and kept going. [[campaign/players/Kaneko\|Kaneko]] watched the arc, calculated, and shot the raven out of the air. Obold fell. [[campaign/players/Ofie\|Ofie]], already in giant hawk form, banked hard and caught him.

Below, [[campaign/npcs/Rotten Agnis Worm Wiggle\|Agnus the Maw]] eventually came down from the wall. She pulsed blight in waves across the room, each pulse washing psychic damage over whoever was closest and shoving them back. It made sustained melee ugly. [[campaign/players/Gunther\|Gunther]] kept attacking regardless.

[[campaign/npcs/Tarian\|Tarian]] — the half-ent who had warned them when they first arrived, the wood elf imprisoned in bark and root for however long the hag had kept him — fought alongside the party. He went down during the exchange. The party pulled him back up. He kept fighting.

The hag's health was failing. The fight was nearly done.

---

### The Kill

[[campaign/players/Ofie\|Ofie]], still in hawk form, banked back toward the lair with [[campaign/players/Obold\|Obold]] on her back. The hag was low. Both of them could see it.

They came in together.

Ofie struck. Obold struck at the same moment.

[[campaign/npcs/Rotten Agnis Worm Wiggle\|Agnus the Maw]] was dead.

Obold will later acknowledge, honestly, that he spent most of the fight attached to a bird. His contribution to the actual hag-killing was one hit at the end. He considers this a fine outcome.

---

### The Pocket Dimension Collapses

The lair did not take the hag's death well.

The walls began to fracture. The wet-flesh ground of the forest started pulling apart. The pocket dimension had been held together by Agnus — with her gone, it was coming undone.

[[campaign/npcs/Tarian\|Tarian]] did not make it out. Whether the collapse took him before the party could reach him, or whether his bound form was tied to the pocket dimension itself, he was lost when the dimension fell.

The party got out.

---

### The Island Sinks

The real world was not much better. As the party emerged from the collapsing pocket dimension, [[campaign/locations/3_nations/Oman\|Oman]] itself was going under — the Witch's Maw draining the island from beneath, water rushing in from every direction.

They got each other out. Not a rout, not a panic — the party moved together, helping and encouraging, pulling anyone who stumbled until everyone was clear of the dimension's threshold.

[[campaign/players/Ofie\|Ofie]] was still in her giant eagle form. The party grabbed the magic rope and tied off to her in a chain — eight people stretched out behind an enormous eagle like a string of very tired, very wet monkeys. [[campaign/players/Obold\|Obold]] uncorked his [[campaign/items/Eversmoking Stone\|eversmoking bottle]] and trailed smoke behind the chain as they moved, marking their path through the chaos for anyone looking.

Then Ofie flew.

What followed was less an escape and more an obstacle course at speed — surging water, debris, trees and wreckage tumbling in the flood. Ofie threaded through it with the chain snapping and swinging behind her, the party doing their best to tuck and angle and not catch a log to the face.

They made open water. The island was still going down behind them.

The [[campaign/items/Bitch's Scorn\|Bitch's Scorn]] was not responding.

[[campaign/players/Alias\|Alias]] reached for the whisper quill and wrote to [[campaign/npcs/Magnolia Duvall\|Magnolia Duvall]]. She responded — she was coming.

Shortly after, the crew checked in. They were safe; the surge had driven them far out to sea, but they were already on their way back.

[[campaign/npcs/Magnolia Duvall\|Mags]] arrived first. She pulled them aboard. The crew arrived not long after. [[campaign/locations/3_nations/Oman\|Oman]] had gone under behind them.

---

### Parting with Mags

Magnolia had somewhere else she needed to be, or somewhere she wanted to go, or both. She and the party exchanged the usual pleasantries — the kind that mean more than they sound — and parted ways again.

The Scorned Eight headed south.

---

### The Voyage South

The party took a night's rest underway, then pushed for [[campaign/npcs/Gwynneth\|Gwynneth]].

- **Day 1:** A storm — middling, not catastrophic. [[campaign/players/Alias\|Alias]] got them most of the way through without incident.
- **Day 2:** A whirlpool, avoided. A cluster of [[campaign/npcs/Gwynneth\|Gwyneth]] patrol ships passed in the distance, watching but not intercepting.
- **Day 3:** Something large came up from the water alongside the ship.

The session ended there.
