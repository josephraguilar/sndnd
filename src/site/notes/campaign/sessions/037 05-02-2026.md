## AI Summary

### The Third Surge

The Witch's Maw did not care that they were moving. The water pulse came again without warning — a deep percussion from somewhere beneath the island's center, the bog dropping away just before the wall of water arrived.

Most of the party reached higher ground. [[campaign/players/Alias\|Alias]] did not. The surge caught him and dragged him along the swamp floor. [[campaign/players/Obold\|Obold]] was already moving — he planted himself, extended [[campaign/npcs/Z'Daria Nix\|Z'Daria Nix]] horizontally at arm's length, and held. Alias grabbed the halberd's shaft. The water tore past and receded. Both of them were still standing.

They kept moving.

---

### The Green Glow

[[campaign/players/Kormak\|Kormak]]'s birds had ranged ahead through the fog on routine patrol when one of them spotted it: a faint green luminescence pulsing somewhere in the mist, directionless at first and then clearly coming from the island's elevated interior. The party adjusted their heading and marched toward it.

What they found first was not the source of the light.

Two trolls loped out of the fog, flanked by lizardfolk handlers armed with barbed-edged blades. The handlers were trying to control the trolls rather than fight alongside them. Whether they had been drawn to the party by sound or scent or the orders of someone watching from above was unclear.

---

### The Patrol Fight

[[campaign/players/Lois\|Lois]] opened with a Fireball that engulfed the cluster of handlers and trolls before they could separate. [[campaign/players/Ofie\|Ofie]] followed immediately, dropping Spike Growth across the approach — dense, thorned ground that punished every step the survivors tried to take toward the party. The combination was devastating. Most of the handlers were dead or dying before the melee could begin.

[[campaign/players/Thorin\|Thorin]] pointed at the remaining combatants and commanded them to grovel. Several did.

[[campaign/players/Gunther\|Gunther]] raged and charged. His wild magic chose that moment to poison him — but the surge turned inward, and the poison fed back into his strikes as bonus damage rather than simply hurting him. He continued forward regardless.

[[campaign/players/Kormak\|Kormak]] painted the surviving group with Faerie Fire, lighting them up for everyone.

[[campaign/players/Obold\|Obold]] misty-stepped into the middle of the fight, uncorked his [[campaign/items/Eversmoking Stone\|ever-smoking bottle]], and let the thick black smoke roll out around him. His intent was to draw the trolls into the darkness where he could fight them on his terms. The trolls did not notice him at all and walked directly past the smoke cloud without changing direction.

Undeterred, Obold attempted to leap over the Spike Growth. He did not clear it. He landed in the middle of the thorns and took extra damage on top of the wounds he'd already accumulated.

A third troll emerged from the bog as the fight ground on. [[campaign/players/Kaneko\|Kaneko]] dropped arrows from range while [[campaign/players/Alias\|Alias]] moved in and out of the melee, drawing the trolls' attention toward the back line and getting them to redirect at Kaneko instead of whoever they'd been targeting.

[[campaign/players/Thorin\|Thorin]] banished one of the trolls mid-fight, removing it cleanly from the battlefield. The party circled the spot where it had been standing and waited. When Thorin released the banishment and the troll snapped back into reality, the Scorned Eight were already surrounding it. The troll did not survive the reunion.

---

### Aftermath

The handlers' barbed blades were stripped and set aside. Looting the bodies turned up **250 obsidian trade tokens** and a gold necklace on each of the fallen, each piece worth roughly **25 gold**. The trolls yielded less — but Obold found what he was looking for. He wrenched one of the troll's tusks free and kept it.

He then summoned **[[campaign/npcs/Ramses\|Ramses VIII]]**.

The party found defensible ground and took a long rest.

---

### The Tower

When they resumed their march, the green glow was no longer ambiguous. It resolved from fog into architecture: a tower rising from the bog, its stones suffused with a cold emerald light that pulsed slowly, like breath. This was [[campaign/npcs/Rotten Agnis Worm Wiggle\|Agnus the Maw]]'s Galder Tower.

They heard the music before they saw the source.

Two **harpies** perched in the fog near the tower's base, and their song reached into the party like fingers. Several members felt the pull — a warm loosening, a sense that stopping here would be fine, that whatever the harpies offered might be worth considering.

The harpies spoke through the music. They said they could take away pain. That there was a price, naturally — a favor, unspecified, to be named later. They also confirmed what the party had been told: [[campaign/npcs/Rotten Agnis Worm Wiggle\|Agnus the Maw]] was inside the tower.

The party tried to negotiate. They wanted an audience with Agnis, not an open-ended debt to her sentries. The harpies' song swelled in response, pulling harder, and for a moment it seemed like the entire party might simply agree to whatever terms were offered.

[[campaign/players/Ofie\|Ofie]] called down lightning on one of the harpies. The music broke.

[[campaign/players/Thorin\|Thorin]] cast Silence across the remaining singers, cutting the song off entirely.

---

### The Drakes

The moment the harpies' charm faltered, riders descended from above. Drake mounts — fast, aggressive, hard to track in the fog — swept low over the party and seized three members, dragging them into the air.

[[campaign/players/Obold\|Obold]] saw [[campaign/players/Lois\|Lois]] lifted off the ground. He misty-stepped directly onto the drake carrying her, appearing astride it in midair. He found the rider, grabbed, and threw the rider clear of the mount. Then he steered the drake into the ground, hard, to bring Lois down safely.

Lois, now back on solid ground, cast Fireball into the remaining harpies and took out most of them in a single burst.

The surviving drakes scattered. Whatever sentries had guarded the tower's approach were gone.

The Scorned Eight stood at the base of [[campaign/npcs/Rotten Agnis Worm Wiggle\|Agnus the Maw]]'s green tower.
