## Log
After the events on the road, we traveled to Grud-Thacks Cradle, where we met a merchant named Merillian Cinderglass. He hired us to act as his protection and muscle while he attempted to negotiate trade between Velen and the **Forest Kingdom of Weldalath.

Before meeting him, we stopped to take care of a few errands — picking up supplies and visiting a local magic shop — before finally joining Marillian to begin the journey.

---

### **Merillian Cinderglass**

Merillian is a well-spoken emissary from the **Forest Kingdom of Weldalath**. He was sent by the king and queen to persuade Ugmarn One Tusk, leader of Grud Thrak Cradle, to open trade relations between their two nations. They require rare materials from Velen and the surrounding lands.

However, Merillian and his men had  been asked to drink **scorpion venom** as part of an orc tradition during trade talks. When they refused, his men were imprisoned leaving him desperate and short-handed. Merillian hoped that we could aid him in talks with Ugmarn and helping release his men, and strengthening Weldalath’s trade position.

- Ugmarn = Orc leader of **Grud Thrak Cradle**

- Marillian’s mission: secure diplomacy and mutual trade routes between **Velen** and **Weldalath**
    

---

### **Grud Thrak Cradle**

Upon arriving at Grud Thrak Cradle, we met Marillian outside its great hall. The orc settlement was a fortified trade post carved into black stone cliffs, surrounded by ash and dust.

Marillian introduced us to Ugmarn and explained his purpose. Ugmarn agreed to speak further — but only if Marillian helped him recover a missing shipment of medicinal herbs from Velen.

---

### **The Hollow Ember Tavern**

During our stay, we spent some time in town. We befriended th e owner of the local tavern, the Hollow Ember, whose name was Ketzrak.

Later, I visited the Cindersink Bathhouse, where I met a half-orc, half-orc woman named Tizra who worked as the bathhouse keeper. After spending the day there, Alias successfully talked her into becoming the tavern’s live-in bard for a night. My performance was apparently convincing enough that she promised to sing if he ever returned to Grud Thrak Cradle.
