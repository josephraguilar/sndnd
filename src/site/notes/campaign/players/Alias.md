# [[campaign/players/Alias\|Alias]]
Alias carries himself with the easy confidence of someone who has talked his way out of more trouble than he has fought his way through. He is the natural face of the party — charming, quick with banter, and disarmingly casual even in dangerous situations, as evidenced by the message he fired off mid-scouting-mission ("About to fuck up some goliaths, hbu?"). In combat he is a precise and elusive swashbuckler, weaving in and out of the melee to land strikes and slip away before enemies can respond. Beneath the bravado sits a genuine leader: even Obold, who defers to no one easily, accepted him as captain of the Bitch's Scorn without protest. He is a follower of Tymora, and her presence — felt as a spinning coin — marks him as someone who trusts in fortune, perhaps more than he trusts in certainty.

---

## Personality
**Traits:**
- Socially magnetic and quick-witted; defaults to charm and banter in tense encounters, whether with a pirate at the Velvet Veil, a half-orc bard at a bathhouse, or a dockmaster looking to collect full fees.
- Elusive and tactically fluid in combat, preferring to dart in for precision strikes and retreat before enemies can close the distance around him.
- Casually competent under pressure — he navigated the ship through multiple storms without apparent anxiety, treating crisis as just another thing to manage.

**Ideals:**
- Fortune favors the bold. As a devotee of Tymora, Alias embraces risk and trusts that luck — earned or otherwise — is a valid strategy.
- Leadership through trust: he was made captain not through force but because the party genuinely believed he was the right person for the role, and he has honored that.

**Bonds:**
- [[campaign/npcs/Xanaphia\|Xanaphia]] — a deep and unresolved personal tie. He went to considerable effort to learn her fate, received a vision of her from Baphomet, and her uncertain whereabouts clearly weigh on him.
- The Bitch's Scorn and its crew — he takes the captaincy seriously, navigating through storms and working to secure a homebase for the party.

**Flaws:**
- His easy, joking manner can make him seem flippant at moments that call for gravity — messaging a contact mid-ambush is charming in retrospect, but speaks to a habit of treating danger casually.
- His faith in Tymora and luck means he may be too comfortable leaving important outcomes to chance rather than forcing certainty when certainty is available.
