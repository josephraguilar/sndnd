# [[campaign/players/Kormak\|Kormak]]
A dwarven artificer who carries himself with quiet competence, Kormak rarely leads from the front but is indispensable to the party's success. He favors information over improvisation — his owl familiar SB is almost always the first thing sent into a dangerous situation — and he has a knack for solving problems that others can't even see coming. Unassuming in conversation, he proves his worth in moments of tactical brilliance, and there is clearly a spiritual life simmering beneath his pragmatic exterior, one that his deity seems to be watching closely.

---

## Personality
**Traits:**
- Methodical and deliberate — prefers scouting with his familiar before committing to action, and thinks several steps ahead of the immediate situation.
- A creative problem-solver under pressure: uses unconventional combinations of spells and abilities (Suggestion paired with Portent dice, Misty Step to cut off escape routes) to turn the tide when brute force alone won't do it.
- Curious and acquisitive about the arcane — willingly climbed into a void-charged crystal chamber and traded in a fey market for a locket he didn't fully understand.

**Ideals:**
- Knowledge and preparation are the most reliable weapons; the right information at the right moment matters more than raw power.
- Faith is real and earned — when his deity manifested as a hammer during Obold's duel, it was confirmation that the divine pays attention to those who act with purpose.

**Bonds:**
- His brother [[campaign/players/Thorin\|Thorin]] is his most immediate family tie and a source of grounding loyalty within the party.
- His owl familiar SB is not merely a tool but an extension of Kormak's will — the first thing he trusts into the unknown.

**Flaws:**
- His instinct to observe and gather information can make him appear passive when the moment calls for boldness; he is sometimes more comfortable behind the scenes than at the center of the fight.
- His curiosity about dangerous artifacts and arcane phenomena occasionally outpaces his caution — he reached for the void crystals alone, and the locket's fey magic could have gone badly wrong.
