# [[campaign/players/Ofie\|Ofie]]
A Firbolg who is said to be the chosen one of prophecy, she stands out by her purple mohawk that is retained in some form on all the animals she transforms into. Ofie moves through the world with quiet attentiveness — noticing the animals, the glyphs, the creatures others overlook — and her instinct is always to seek understanding before reaching for force. She is a powerful and versatile druid whose Wild Shape is her signature, shifting into bears, wolves, sharks, eagles, and more as the moment demands, and her comfort among beasts is matched only by her facility with the druidic traditions her people carry. Though she can be devastating in combat, she is equally capable of ending a confrontation entirely through gentleness, whether calming an owlbear with goodberries, scratching a sea turtle's neck, or talking her way past a threat with well-placed deception. Beneath the practicality, Ofie carries the weight of a prophecy she has not yet fully accepted — a vision of herself crowned in flowers, bound to the promised land of Lebor Gabala — and that destiny seems to reach for her even when she is not looking for it.

## Personality
**Traits:**
- Prefers peaceful solutions and leads with empathy toward animals and creatures; will attempt communication, appeasement, or deception before committing to violence.
- Perceptive and knowledgeable — reads ancient druidic scripts, identifies flags and lore from memory, and picks up on environmental details (scents, sounds, tracks) that others miss.
- Practical and adaptable in combat, shifting forms to match what the fight needs: summoning wolves for chaos, charging in as a bear, soaring as an eagle to rescue allies.

**Ideals:**
- The natural world and its creatures deserve care and respect; Ofie consistently acts to protect, free, or aid animals even in the middle of a battle.
- The old ways of the Firbolg — keeper of forgotten myths, protector of nature — are a sacred charge, not merely a heritage.

**Bonds:**
- Bound by destiny to the Firbolg prophecy of Lebor Gabala; Baphomet's vision of her crowned in flowers on Mintarin suggests a purpose she has not yet fully stepped into.
- Has developed trust and camaraderie with the party, particularly with Kaneko, with whom she often scouts and watches for threats together.

**Flaws:**
- Her commitment to precise, conditional orders (wolves must not attack women and children) can cause hesitation or tactical complications mid-battle when reality doesn't match the plan.
- Can be self-deprecating or self-preserving under pressure — after fumbling a leap off a cliff in bear form, she went invisible rather than rejoin the fight.
